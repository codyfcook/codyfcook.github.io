Sources of Inefficiency in Scientific Research

with Maya Durvasula
[abstract]
Across fields and subfields of science, nearly all research effort is concentrated on a small fraction of potential topics. In this paper, we investigate whether patterns such as these are indicators of inefficiency. A theoretical model highlights three factors that can induce, or exacerbate, inefficiency in scientific investment: knowledge spillovers, path dependence, and heterogeneous scientist ability. We show that, together, these mechanisms can distort new investment toward well-studied research domains. Newly- constructed data and novel measurement strategies allow us to test for and quantify these distortions empirically. We develop and estimate a quantitative model of scientific investment, which allows us to examine the distribution of scientific research investments under various counterfactuals.
